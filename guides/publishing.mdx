Publishing in Frappe Wiki controls which content is visible to end users. This guide covers publication settings, visibility control, and best practices for managing published content.

## Understanding Publication Status

### Published vs. Unpublished

Every page has a publication status:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Published Pages">
    **Characteristics:**
    - Visible to all users with access to the wiki
    - Appear in navigation and search
    - Accessible via direct URL
    - Included in sitemap for SEO

    **Use for:** Complete, reviewed content ready for users
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Unpublished Pages">
    **Characteristics:**
    - Only visible to editors and wiki managers
    - Don't appear in public navigation
    - Not indexed by search engines
    - Still accessible to team members

    **Use for:** Work in progress, incomplete content, or internal documentation
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### How Publishing Works

Publication is managed through two levels:

1. **Page-Level Publishing**: Individual pages can be published or unpublished
2. **Space-Level Publishing**: Entire wiki spaces can be public or private

<Info>
Both the space and the page must be published for content to be visible to end users.
</Info>

## Setting Page Publication Status

### For New Pages

When creating a page:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Page Dialog">
    In the new page dialog, look for the **Published** checkbox.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Initial Status">
    - ✅ **Checked**: Page will be published after merge (default)
    - ⬜ **Unchecked**: Page stays unpublished after merge
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Create the page. The status is saved in your draft.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### For Existing Pages

Toggle publication status:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Page Menu">
    Right-click the page in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Toggle Status">
    Select **Publish** or **Unpublish** from the menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save Draft">
    The change is saved in your draft change request.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Merge to Apply">
    Merge your change request to apply the publication status change.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Publication status changes go through the same review workflow as content edits. Changes take effect only after merging.
</Note>

## Visual Indicators

### In the Sidebar

Pages show their status with visual cues:

- **Normal text color**: Published page
- **Dimmed text**: Unpublished page
- **Orange "Not Published" badge**: Unpublished page indicator

### In Drafts

When working in a change request:
- Pages being published show no special indicator
- Pages being unpublished show the "Not Published" badge

## Group Publication Behavior

### How Groups are Published

Groups (folders) have different publication rules:

- **Groups don't have publish status**: They're automatically visible if they contain published pages
- **Empty groups**: Hidden from public view
- **Groups with only unpublished pages**: Hidden from public view
- **Groups with at least one published page**: Visible in navigation

### Navigating to Groups

When users click a published group:
- They're redirected to the first published page within that group
- If no published pages exist, the group appears empty (but shouldn't be visible)

## Space-Level Publishing

### Wiki Space Publication

Control entire space visibility:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Space Settings">
    Navigate to the wiki space list and click the space's settings icon.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Toggle Space Publishing">
    Find the **Is Published** checkbox:
    - ✅ **Published**: Space is public (if server allows)
    - ⬜ **Unpublished**: Space is private to team members
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save** to apply the change immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Unpublishing a space makes all its pages inaccessible to public users, regardless of individual page publication status.
</Warning>

### Private Pages Within Public Spaces

For granular access control:

1. Keep the space published
2. Unpublish individual pages that should be internal
3. Use groups to organize public vs. private content

## Publication Workflow

### Standard Publishing Flow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Content">
    Write pages in your draft change request.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Publication Status">
    Mark pages as published or unpublished based on completion.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review">
    Submit the change request for review.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Merge">
    After approval, merge the change request.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Content Goes Live">
    Published pages immediately appear in the live wiki.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Unpublishing Existing Content

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Draft">
    Start editing the wiki (creates a draft automatically).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Unpublish Pages">
    Right-click pages and select **Unpublish**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Request Review">
    Submit for review if required.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Merge">
    Merge the change request to unpublish the pages.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pages Hidden">
    Unpublished pages disappear from public view immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
Unpublished pages remain in your wiki's structure and can be republished anytime.
</Info>

## Managing Drafts and Publication

### Draft Content Visibility

While working in a draft:
- All pages are visible in the editor (regardless of publish status)
- Publication status is indicated by badges
- Preview reflects what will be published after merge

### Bulk Publication

To publish multiple pages at once:

1. Open your draft
2. Navigate through pages in the sidebar
3. Right-click and publish each one
4. Merge to apply all changes together

<Note>
There's no "Publish All" button. Each page must be published individually to ensure intentional publication decisions.
</Note>

## SEO and Discoverability

### Search Engine Visibility

Published pages:
- Are indexed by search engines (if wiki is public)
- Include meta tags for SEO
- Generate sitemap entries
- Show in search results

Unpublished pages:
- Have `noindex` meta tags
- Are excluded from sitemaps
- Won't appear in search results
- Redirect non-editors away

### Internal Search

**For Editors:**
- Both published and unpublished pages appear in wiki search
- Status badges show publication state

**For Users:**
- Only published pages appear in search results
- Unpublished pages are filtered out

## Access Control

### Public Access

For publicly accessible wikis:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Guest Users">
    - Can view published pages
    - Cannot view unpublished pages
    - Cannot access edit functionality
    - Cannot see drafts or change requests
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Logged-In Users">
    - Can view all published pages
    - May see unpublished pages (if granted permission)
    - May have edit access (role-dependent)
    - Can view their own drafts
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Private Pages

Use the **Private** flag for sensitive content:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Edit Page Settings">
    Open the page and click the route to access settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable Private Flag">
    Check the **Private** checkbox.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and Merge">
    Save the settings and merge your change request.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Private pages:**
- Require users to be logged in
- Don't appear for guest users
- May require specific roles for access
- Useful for internal documentation

## Common Publishing Patterns

### Progressive Publication

```
1. Create pages as unpublished
2. Work on multiple pages in drafts
3. Publish completed pages incrementally
4. Keep WIP pages unpublished until ready
```

**Benefits:**
- Allows work on large documentation sets
- Publishes content as it's completed
- Avoids overwhelming users with incomplete info

### Staged Publishing

```
1. Create entire section as unpublished
2. Review and finalize all pages
3. Publish entire section at once
4. Launch with complete, cohesive content
```

**Benefits:**
- Ensures complete coverage before launch
- Provides consistent user experience
- Good for feature launches or major updates

### Internal + External Content

```
Root
├── Public Docs (published)
│   ├── User Guide (published)
│   └── Tutorials (published)
└── Internal Docs (unpublished)
    ├── Architecture Notes (unpublished)
    └── Team Processes (unpublished)
```

**Benefits:**
- Single wiki for all documentation
- Clear separation between public and internal
- Easy to maintain and navigate

## Publication Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Default to Published">
    Create pages as published unless there's a specific reason not to. Users expect documentation to be visible.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Review Before Publishing">
    Always review content before publishing, especially for public wikis. Check for accuracy, completeness, and formatting.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use Unpublished for WIP">
    Mark incomplete pages as unpublished to avoid confusing users with partial information.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Publish Complete Sections">
    When adding new sections, publish all related pages together for a complete user experience.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Update Publication Status Regularly">
    Periodically review unpublished pages and publish them when complete, or delete if no longer needed.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Document Publication Decisions">
    Use change request descriptions to explain why pages are published or unpublished.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Test Before Publishing">
    Preview pages in the draft view to ensure they look correct before making them public.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting Publication Issues

### Page Not Appearing Publicly

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check Page Status">
    Verify the page is marked as published (no "Not Published" badge).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Space Status">
    Ensure the wiki space itself is published.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Private Flag">
    If the page is private, you must be logged in to view it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify Merge">
    Confirm the change request containing the publication change was merged.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Parent Groups">
    Ensure the page is in a group that contains other published pages.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Page Appearing When It Shouldn't

<Steps>
  <Step title="Verify Unpublish Action">
    Check that you unpublished the page (not just marked it as draft).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm Merge">
    Ensure the change request with the unpublish action was merged.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Caching">
    Clear your browser cache and refresh the page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify Space Settings">
    Confirm the space publication settings match your intent.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Publication and Change Requests

### Publication in CRs

Publication status changes work like content edits:

- Changes are saved in your draft
- Must go through review (if required)
- Take effect after merge
- Can be reverted in a new CR

### Reviewing Publication Changes

When reviewing a CR with publication changes:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check Change Summary">
    Look for pages being published or unpublished in the changes list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify Readiness">
    For pages being published, verify content is complete and accurate.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Consistency">
    Ensure publication status is consistent with the page's completion level.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve or Request Changes">
    Approve if publication decisions are appropriate, or request changes if pages aren't ready.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Monitoring Published Content

### Published Page Count

Track how much content is public:

1. View the wiki sidebar
2. Published pages appear in normal text
3. Count visible pages without "Not Published" badges

### Unpublished Content Report

Periodically review unpublished pages:

1. Create a draft
2. Navigate through the tree
3. Note pages with "Not Published" badges
4. Decide to publish, update, or delete each one
